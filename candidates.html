Medical Doctors

As a medical doctor or physician, you diagnose and treat your patients for all sorts of ailments, or also refer them to the appropriate specialist doctor when needed, for specific medical opinions and advice.

Your major concern is promoting, maintaining, or restoring health through the study, diagnosis, prognosis and treatment of disease, injury, and other physical and mental impairments.

Nurses

Your profession involves years of training and continued specialised education to care for patients in a variety of settings. You are often the first healthcare professional that patients meet. You are a technical expert, an educator, a counsellor and a resource for the family, using all senses to better care for a patient.

You focus on alleviating pain and suffering through protection, promoting health, wellness, and prevention of illness and injury. Your day-to-day is an advocacy in the care of individuals, families, communities, and populations. You advocate for education, prevention, collaboration, coordination of care, all while adhering to a high standard of care.

Allied Health Professionals

You’re an health professional who apply your expertise to prevent disease transmission, diagnose, treat and rehabilitate people of all ages and all specialties.

You play a crucial role in helping patients live life as fully as possible. You work as a part of a central team in emergency response and diagnosis to mental and physical rehabilitation.

Together with a range of technical and support staff you may deliver direct patient care, rehabilitation, treatment, diagnostics and health improvement interventions to restore and maintain optimal physical, sensory, psychological, cognitive and social functions.
